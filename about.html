Learn more about us!

Our charity organisation helps stray cats around Europe to find a loving home and a caring family.

Our Organisation

We are Mao, a charity organisation that helps cats around Europe. Our shelter provides rescued cats all the attention and care they need to live a happy life. We started as a small team, motivated by creating a safe environment for cats. At the current moment we activate in multiple countries around Europe, and with help from caring people we can continue growing and doing our work.

We started our journey in 2017, with a small team of 7 people. Over time our team grew and we crossed the borders. We now have associates in multiple cities from Bulgaria, Romania, Ukraine, Hungary, Austria, Germany, France, United Kingdom, Poland, and Czech Republic. We constantly provide food, shelter, and treatments for cats in need. We also offer cats for adoption, and always provide the most simple way for you to help them.

Our Motivation

We are motivated to help stray cats because we believe in giving these vulnerable creatures a better quality of life. Seeing them in distress and suffering drives us to take action and provide them with food, shelter, and medical care. The satisfaction of knowing that we can make a difference in their lives and give them a chance at a happier and healthier future is truly rewarding and keeps us committed to helping them however we can.

Our Vision

Helping stray cats can make the world a better place by reducing their suffering and improving their quality of life. By providing them with food, shelter, and medical care, we can save them from starvation, illness, and injury. In doing so, we also prevent overpopulation, reduce the spread of diseases, and promote a sense of compassion in our communities. Ultimately, creating a safer and more humane environment for all living beings.

The future of helping stray cats

The future of helping stray cats looks promising as more organizations and individuals are becoming active in rescuing, fostering, and finding homes for these animals. With advancements in technology, such as social media platforms and online fundraising tools, awareness and support for stray cat populations are increasing. Additionally, there is a growing trend towards trap-neuter-return programs to control the overpopulation of strays and improve their quality of life. Overall, with continued efforts and a community-driven approach, the future of helping stray cats seems hopeful in finding sustainable solutions for their welfare.

FAQ
Q: Can I adopt a cat if I already have another cat at home?
A: Sure! Some cats we have grew up getting in contact with other cats and they are very friendly and curious. If your cat also accepts another cat then there is no problem. You have also the option to schedule a visit at our centre and bring your cat as well. This is the best way to check if they will get along well.
Q: I live in a house with garden. Can the cat I adopted stay outside over night?
A: We recommend that you know all the time where your cat is. Cats that live outside the home can run away or get in trouble with other cats or animals. This is why we don't recommend letting your cat stay outside without supervision from you. You can learn your cat to wear a leash and go for a walk outside together. If you have any question about this, please contact us and we will do our best to help you with advice.
Q: Can I adopt a cat from your shelters even if I don't live in the coutries you activate in?
A: Of course, but you will need to take a trip to one of the countries we have associates in (Bulgaria, Romania, Ukraine, Hungary, Austria, Germany, France, UK, Poland, or Czech Republic), in order to take the cat home. We can schedule a call and show you the cats we have for adoption. Once you agree on a cat, we will make all the documents and formalities necessary for the trip (passport, chip, vaccines, etc.), free of charge. Please contact us through the contact form or give us a call at +1234567890. We will do our best to help you with the adoption process.
Q: How can I cancel my monthly donation subscription?
A: It is very simple to cancel the subscription. Just leave a message in the contact form adding your email, and one of our team members will get in contact with you and cancel the subscription with no delay.
Q: I am aware of a family of stray cats that are in need of food and care. Can I get in contact with you about this?
A: Of course! Please contact us as soon as possible contact form or call us at +1234567890. If you live in one of the countries we rescue cats from (Bulgaria, Romania, Ukraine, Hungary, Austria, Germany, France, UK, Poland, or Czech Republic) we will get in contact with our associates there and organize ourselves to help.
Q: Can I schedule my cat for a vaccine appointment at your centre?
A: Of course! We provide aid regarding vaccinations, treatments and special food for your cat. Just for your information, all of these need to be payed by you. Please check the opening hours and schedule an appointment in our contact form or call us at +1234567890.
Q: Do you have a veterinarian that can check my cat?
A: Yes, we can help you with that! Since we rescue and treat cats, we are always in contact with veterinarians that can offer a medical check for your cat. Just for your information, the appointment needs to be payed by you. Please check the opening hours and schedule an appointment in our contact form or call us at +1234567890.
